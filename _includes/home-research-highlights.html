My first signature method, Ordered Network Analysis (ONA), has been employed in 100+ published works across fields such as quantitative ethnography, learning analytics, AI in education, healthcare, and nursing education, since my colleagues and I introduced it in 2023 (ONA method paper). ONA was recognized as Best Student Paper at the 2022 International Conference on Quantitative Ethnography.

My current work, Epistemic Trajectory Modeling (ETM), extends ONA to model learning trajectories. Rather than summarizing learning as a static network, this new method traces the underlying mechanisms that drive behavior change over time and describes learning as a dynamic process as it actually unfolds. In addition, I investigate adjacent topics that inform and enrich this methods work:

Visualization design. I believe that the alignment between the numerical and visual representation of models is key to interpretation. I’ve written about visual design principles and graph layout choices that support faithful and intuitive reading of network analysis results.

Simulations. iPlan is an urban planning simulation that dynamically models land-use scenarios across the contiguous United States. I developed an approach that uses simulated play to construct a normative measurement space, a model of expert behavior against which learner activity can be projected and assessed, providing a principled basis for scaffolding learning in open-ended game environments (Thin Data, Thick Description).
