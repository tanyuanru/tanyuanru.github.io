<h2 id="teaching-overview" class="teaching-page__pillar">Teaching</h2>

<section class="teaching-block" aria-labelledby="teaching-undergrad-heading">
  <h3 id="teaching-undergrad-heading" class="teaching-subhead">Undergraduate teaching</h3>
  <p>I taught <strong>EDPSYCH 301 How People Learn</strong>, a large undergraduate lecture course, for three semesters (Spring 2025, Fall 2025, Spring 2026). In this course, we explore foundational cognitive and sociocultural learning theories and their applications in educational psychology. In this class we have a <a href="https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1kuxEOxmBrjQ17s2QKsQpeB_zjSFWkTfxCUxl_NLQfwo/edit?gid=0#gid=0" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">shared resource spreadsheet</a>, where students contribute study tools, playlists, favorite campus spots, and quotes that kept them going. It’s a simple way to remind each other that learning is deeply human. With students’ permission, we keep it growing each semester. You’re welcome to take a look; something in there might brighten your day.</p>
</section>

<section class="teaching-block" aria-labelledby="teaching-graduate-heading">
  <h3 id="teaching-graduate-heading" class="teaching-subhead">Graduate teaching</h3>
  <p>I worked as a teaching assistant for three courses in the <a href="https://learninganalytics.education.wisc.edu/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Master of Science in Learning Analytics</a> at the University of Wisconsin–Madison: EDPSYCH 575 Instructional Design for Learning Analytics, EDPSYCH 615 Conversations and Visualizations, and EDPSYCH 695 Capstone in Learning Analytics. This online master’s program helps students develop data analysis skills and learn how to bring those insights to life through data visualization. I have taught all three courses twice; I supported students from design thinking through hands-on work with tools like Tableau and R, and through capstone projects where they work with real data from real clients.</p>
</section>

<section class="teaching-block" aria-labelledby="teaching-professional-heading">
  <h3 id="teaching-professional-heading" class="teaching-subhead">Professional training</h3>
  <p>I have been a facilitator for the <a href="https://www.qefellows.org/program/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">QE Fellows Institute</a>, an NSF-funded program that introduces researchers to quantitative ethnography (QE). Fellows are typically early-career faculty in education and related fields, along with researchers and practitioners outside traditional academia. Each summer, cohorts come to UW–Madison for a week-long intensive workshop in July. In my sessions I teach epistemic network analysis (ENA), ordered network analysis (ONA), and automated coding. Across the institute, fellows also build skill in designing and conducting QE studies, collaborating on interdisciplinary teams, mentoring colleagues, and leading QE workshops at their home institutions. I served as a facilitator from 2023–2026.</p>
</section>
